Rationale

The number and impact of natural, technological and human-made disasters and emergencies are rising in Canada and globally. As a result, demands for HQPs in disaster and emergency management profession and relevant industries have sharply increased in recent years and will continue to grow in the future. According to market analysts (Marketsandmarkets.com), the global market for public safety and emergency management products and services is expected to grow from $415.53 billion in 2013 to $544.02 billion by 2018. However, Canada's share in this industry, especially in the information technology related section, is very minimal. This is in part due to the lack of organized and coordinated training and research in this area.
Our ADERSIM trainees will enhance Canada's human resources and scientific and engineering capacities in this rapidly growing industry. They will be able to develop advanced, intelligent, agent-based real-time computer simulations, which play an important role in disaster and emergency mitigation, preparedness, response, and recovery planning and management.

Training Program Objectives

The long term goal of ADERSIM is to enhance Canada's scientific and technological capacities in public safety and emergency management through innovative training, research, and development of professionals in state-of-the-art simulation and emergency management systems. In the short-term, the ADERSIM program will: 1) train a large number of the next generation HQPs with interdisciplinary, theoretical, technical, and professional skills in advanced disaster and emergency modeling, simulations and information management to meet Canada's and global demands in emergency management and public safety industry; 2) develop and test a set of new, high-tech, low-cost, scalable, and user friendly disaster and emergency simulation tools and technologies in close partnership with a large number of Canadian industry partners and a wide range of end user organizations; 3) provide trainees with rich and unique educational experiences, professional activities, career development, knowledge transformation and mobilization, and leadership skills in industry, government and academia through customized curriculums, internships with industry and end users, student exchanges, summer schools, and student exchange programs with national and international partners.

Training Program Components and Activities

Developing successful disaster and emergency simulation tools requires backgrounds in four key areas: subject disaster risk (flood, earthquake, hurricane, industrial accidents, etc.), disaster and emergency management functions (search and rescue, disaster logistics, evacuation, etc.), disaster and emergency information management (spatial and non-spatial), and mathematical modeling and computer simulation. Students recruited to the ADERSIM program should have strong background in at least two areas. Participants of the ADERSIM program will be trained to conduct coordinated, synchronized and complimentary research and development in disaster simulations using our network of interconnected labs including the cutting edge Disaster and Emergency Simulation Facility at York University.

Future Career Possibilities for Trainees

The ADERSIM program facilitates the transition of trainees into the workforce that includes academia, industry, government, and non-governmental organizations. PDF and PhD trainees will be able to find jobs in a growing academic environment related to public safety and emergency management. While more disaster and emergency management programs are needed, trained professionals to teach and research are very limited. Trainees with a master's degree will join industry, government, or non-governmental organizations. Those with knowledge and skills in developing disaster information systems and simulation solutions will either join existing companies or will create their own companies. Some trainees are expected to join their internship companies and organizations and other large corporations that use technology and disaster and emergency simulations tools (i.e. airports, hydro companies, transportation companies, oil and gas companies, major banks and insurance). Moreover, large governmental and non-governmental organizations at federal and provincial levels also need experts in disaster and emergency management with IT and simulation expertise.
We anticipate that an interdisciplinary knowledge of disaster and emergency management, computer science, and information technology, management science, and mathematical modeling will provide our trainees with the knowledge and technical skills required to effectively work in one of the above organizations. However, having exposed and gained specific knowledge of the type of organizations and the type of disasters and emergencies that they are faced will be essential for our trainees. We believe that our trainees will get those skills and knowledge through internships and interactions that they have with partner organizations. As soon as our trainees join the program we will ask them to become a student member of professional associations (such as the International Association of Emergency Management and the Society for Modeling & Simulation International) in order to engage them with the professionals and experts in these fields. We also encourage our trainees to take part in relevant courses offered by government agencies and GIS software companies (such as courses offered by the government of Ontario on Incident Management System, ESRI on Advance GIS, and AnyLogic on agent based simulations).
